Is Mars Habitable For Mankind ?

Currently, Mars is not naturally habitable for humans — it’s too cold, has very little oxygen, and no liquid water on the surface. The atmosphere is thin and mostly made of carbon dioxide, which humans can’t breathe. However, scientists are studying Mars closely because it has some ingredients for life: frozen water underground, seasons like Earth, and signs that it once had rivers and lakes. With future technology, we might be able to build special shelters, grow food in greenhouses, and create breathable air — making it possible to live on Mars one day. So while it’s not ready yet, Mars could be a home for humans in the future with the right tools and preparation.
